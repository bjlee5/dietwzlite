Your body needs carbohydrates as a source of energy, but too many carbs is unhealthy. The DGA recommends you keep your daily carbs intake in the range of 45 to 55% of your daily total calories intake. And, go for healthy carb sources that include other important nutrients, such as whole grains, vegetables, fruits and beans.

You can treat yourself to an unhealthy carbs choice once in a while â€“ but limit this behavior. (And keep the unhealthy snacks out-of-sight!)

Examples of things to avoid include:

  • Pastries, donuts, cake, cookies
  • White (refined) bread
  • White rice
  • Sugar and added sugars (like high-fructose corn syrup)

Total carbohydrates on a nutrition label is often the total of sugars, fiber and other carbohydrates. Read food labels to help control your carbs.

Each gram of carbs is 4 calories.
