Dinner in a Mexican Restaurant can be a calorie fiesta, but if you read the menu carefully you can find plenty of healthy options that are low in fat and nutritious. Avoid dishes that are fried or loaded with cheese and sour cream.

Many Mexican restaurants greet patrons with a basket of tortilla chips, salsa and the margarita drink menu. Just 12 chips and a little salsa are about 300 to 350 calories. Margaritas can range from 170 calories (small, 4 oz., on the rocks) to more than 500 calories for a big (10 oz.), sugary, slushy drink. Think about your overall calories goal for the meal . . .

Look for meats and seafood that are grilled and combined with tomatoes, onions, peppers and other vegetables. Avoid dishes where the shrimp or other seafood ingredients are battered and fried.

Stews and casseroles made with chorizo (a spicy pork sausage) are fine as long as cheese and sour cream are limited.

Good Choices:

  • Frijoles (beans) and arroz (rice) - nutritious sources of fiber

  • Bean burrito topped with red or green chili sauce, or pico de gallo (vegetable relish)

  • Chicken enchiladas and beef tacos - made with corn tacos, stuffed with meats or vegetables and topped with a light sprinkle of cheese

  • Fajitas - a combination of grilled meat and fresh vegetables with a small amount of cheese. Avoid sour cream and creamy cheese sauces.

  • Corn tortillas - lower in fat and sodium than flour tortillas

  • Tamales - various fillings sealed in corn husks and steamed

  • Tomato salsa, - nutritious and low in fat and calories

  • Guacamole - contains high-calorie fat, but it is heart-healthy unsaturated fat. Good choice IF consumed in reasonable amounts.

  • Taco salad - without the fried shell and sour cream

  • Soft tacos - rather than hard, fried tacos

  • Calabicitas or colache - a combination of cooked zucchini, corn, peppers and onions

Avoid:

  • Chimichangas and chili rellenos. Both are fried (chimichangas are deep fried) and stuffed with cheese among other ingredients.

  • Eat small portions of Mexican desserts including churros (a fried dough pastry), emapanadas de dulce (sweet turnovers) and polvorones (butter cookies rolled in confectioners sugar).

  • Fried ice cream
