Whether it’s an upscale restaurant or a fast-food place, two of the biggest challenges with restaurant selections are that the portions are too large and they contain too much sodium. In addition, meals are often centered on a large piece of meat, such as a steak, and a starchy side dish such as potatoes, noodles or rice.

To make healthier restaurant choices:

  • Choose small or medium portions for main dishes, side dishes and appetizers.

  • Choose entrées where meat doesn’t overwhelm the plate and is balanced with vegetables and other ingredients.

  • Soups (broth-based), stews and casseroles (not loaded with cheese) are well-balanced options.

  • Select vegetables and salads over potatoes and pastas when there is a choice of side dishes.

  • Order an appetizer or a side dish for your main course instead of an entrée.

  • Choose foods prepared with flavorful herbs and spices, and don't add salt at the table.

  • Share a main dish with a dining companion.

  • Order from the menu instead of heading to the all-you-can-eat buffet.

  • Ignore the “clean-plate club” rule - when you’ve eaten enough, leave the rest.

  • Take leftovers home in a “doggy bag” -- if you can chill the food right away.

  • Ask that foods be prepared without adding salt.

  • Order salad dressings "on-the-side" so that YOU control the amount you consume.

  • Ask for whole-grain breads, cereals and pastas. Try some new grains such as quinoa, barley and amaranth.
