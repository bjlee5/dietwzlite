Daily Exercise = 1 point

Daily exercise is good for everyone! Just 20 to 30 minutes of exercise per day provides overall health benefits.

If you’re physically able, get out there and try for 30 minutes of exercise every day. If you have physical limitations or are overweight or obese, work with your doctor or a trainer to get started slowly.

For people trying to lose weight, some experts recommend daily exercise that burns 300 calories in 30 to 45 minutes.

It takes protein to maintain and build muscle. Normally, protein intake should be between 0.8 and 1.0 g per kg of body weight. 0.8 is a good general target. The "U.S. Department of Health and Human Services and U.S. Department of Agriculture. 2015 – 2020 Dietary Guidelines for Americans, 8th Edition, December 2015" recommends
46 g daily for adult females
56 g for adult males.

However, if you’re very active physically, you’ll need more protein. This is a good reason to consult a registered dietitian (RD or RDN) or your doctor, as physical activity can increase your daily needs to a range of 1.0 to 1.4 g per kg body weight for recreational athletes, and as high as 1.7 for endurance athletes or someone doing regular strength training.

Also, if you're obese (BMI 30 or higher) and are trying to lose weight, you should consult your doctor or a registered dietitian about possibly increasing your protein intake. Some studies show that increased protein in your diet can help satisfy your appetite.
