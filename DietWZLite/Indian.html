Good Choices:

  • Tandoori - items made in a traditional clay oven called a tandoor. Tandoori chicken or shrimp are low in fat and calories.

  • Vegetarian dishes such as dal, made with lentils, and chole, made with chick peas, are low in oil and butter. These legumes are high in fiber and protein. Dai Bukhara and Pindi Chole are example of these dishes.

  • Chicken Tikka with rice instead of Chicken Korma which includes cream and coconut.

  • Kebab dishes are grilled, such as Prawn Kebab and Barrah (lamb) Kebab.

  • Roti - a whole-wheat bread

  • Lentil soup

  • Low-fat plain yogurt is a good option to cool a curry that's too spicy for your taste.

Avoid:

  • Paneer, ghee or malai. Paneers are cheeses, ghee is buttery and malai dishes use cream. Makhani dishes use ghee. Naan may also be brushed with ghee.

  • Naan - a traditional bread served with Indian meals, is delicious but low in nutrition. (Hard to resist - limit how much you eat.)

  • Deep-fried samosas - triangular pastries filled with vegetables, meat or a combination of both

  • Pakoda or Pakora which is fried with tempura batter

  • Limit rice. One cup of basmati rice contains about 200 calories. For some fiber, go with brown rice instead.
