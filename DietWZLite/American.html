American cuisine is a blend of many cultures adapted to foods found, grown and produced in the United States. Americans have many options when it comes to choosing what to eat. Unfortunately, people don’t always make the best choices and often eat too much.

Good Choices:

  • Fresh fruit

  • Fresh vegetables, raw or lightly cooked, served without loads of butter or high-fat sauces.

  • Lean steaks, chops and poultry served grilled or baked.

  • Whole-grain cereals, breads and pasta.

  • Fish and seafood (aim for two meals per week) served grilled, baked or poached with herbs and light sauces.

  • Fat-free or low-fat milk, yogurt, ice cream and other diary products.

Avoid:

  • Deep-fried food. In America, if it's edible, someone has tried to deep fry it. Stay away from deep-fried turkey, hot dogs, candy bars, cookies and any other "county fair" food.

  • Creamy, high-fat salad dressings that contain mayonnaise.

  • High-fat meats such as pork spare ribs or meats served with rich sauces.

Here are some popular American dishes and suggestions for making them healthier choices:

  • Apple pie: Serve without ice cream, whipped cream or cheddar cheese. Eat only part of the pie crust. Alternatives are apple crisp or a fresh raw apple.

  • Bacon, lettuce and tomato sandwich: Use turkey bacon and low-fat mayonnaise, and ask for it served on whole-wheat bread.

  • Barbecued pork ribs : Substitute pulled pork with fat removed.

  • Brownies : Choose a small piece of chocolate or a small chocolate chip cookie.

  • Chili : Avoid toppings such as cheese, sour cream and corn chips.

  • Corn dog : Skip the deep-fried, county fair food and choose a beef or turkey hot dog on a bun.

  • Deep-fried food : Choose items that are grilled, baked or lightly cooked in oil.

  • Doughnut : Choose a small “doughnut hole.”

  • French fries : Substitute baked or boiled potatoes.

  • Fried chicken : Ask for grilled or baked.

  • Hamburger; Cheeseburger : Forget calorie-laden toppings such as special sauces, bacon, extra cheese, etc. Choose plain burgers with vegetables like lettuce, onions, tomatoes or pickles. If you really want cheese, use a thin slice or sprinkle with shredded cheese.

  • Macaroni and Cheese : Choose it as a small side dish or substitute hot pasta tossed with olive oil and garlic, and sprinkled with Parmesan cheese. Add cooked vegetables to increase the nutrition.

  • Pumpkin pie : Skip the whipped topping.

  • Reuben sandwich : Substitute turkey for the corned beef, sweet pickle relish for the Thousand Island dressing and ask for a thin slice of cheese. Ask that it be toasted without butter.

  • Tuna fish sandwich or melt : Ask for whole-wheat bread and low-fat mayonnaise. On the melt, skip the cheese or ask that only a thin slice be used.

  • Wedge salad : Substitute a small, green mixed vegetable salad with dressing on the side and no croutons or bacon.
