Although Italian restaurant menus often contain dishes that are lavished with cheese and high-calorie sauces served in huge portions, there are many healthy alternatives in Italian cuisine. Olive oil, tomatoes, garlic, spinach, radicchio, figs, peppers and beans are among the good-for-you ingredients found in Italian dishes.

Good Choices:

  • Appetizers like minestrone soup, wedding soup, pasta e fagioli (broth-based soup made with pasta and beans)

  • Grilled calamari (not fried)

  • Prosciutto (ham) with melon

  • Caponata - eggplant, onions, tomatoes, anchovies olives, pine nuts and vinegar cooked and served as a relish at room temperature

  • Bruschetta (Italian bread slices) topped with a light mixture of tomatoes and herbs

  • Red tomato sauces instead of white creamy cheese sauces

  • Salads (insalata) that are mostly greens tossed with a light blend of olive oil and red wine or balsamic vinegar

  • Caprese salad - a combination of tomatoes, fresh basil leaves and fresh mozzarella seasoned with salt and pepper and drizzled with fresh olive oil

  • Panzanella (Italian bread salad) - a combination of chunks of bread, onions, tomatoes, basil, olive oil, vinegar and seasonings

  • Cacciatore or pizzaiola entrees where the meat is simply browned in olive oil then simmered with tomatoes and a variety of other vegetables and seasonings

  • Piccata-style dishes - thin slices of veal or chicken saut√©ed and served with a sauce made from pan juices, lemon juice and parsley

  • Cheese or meat ravioli topped with a marinara sauce

  • Pasta with a red sauce and a light topping of grated Parmesan cheese

  • Risotto made from rice, can be a good choice if it is not loaded with cheese and other high-fat ingredients

  • For desserts, go with small servings of granita (Italian ice), gelato (Italian ice cream), or biscotti, a low-fat cookie that is dipped in coffee or wine to soften before eating

Avoid:

  • Antipasto - a selection of meats, cheeses, olives and marinated vegetables that is high in sodium and can fill you up before the main course arrives

  • Any dish that says Alfredo or carbonara (creamy, high-fat sauces loaded with calories)

  • Parmigiana dishes, which are breaded, fried and topped with cheese

  • Lasagna has layers of heavy cheese and creamy sauce with lots of fat and calories

  • Fried ravioli - too many calories

  • When ordering pizza, cut calories by choosing thin crusts, mostly vegetable toppings and a minimum of cheese. Limit your servings. Calzones are simply pizza crust and fillings folded to make a turnover, so the same rules apply.

  • Gorgonzola cheese sauce - very rich

  • Bread sticks and garlic bread slices slathered with butter and buried under a layer of cheese. Better choice: plain Italian bread dipped in a small amount of flavored olive oil.

  • Desserts like tiramisu, panna cotta and cannoli
