Each serving = NEGATIVE 1 point

OK, maybe you don’t want to completely deprive yourself of sweet snacks – or you may overeat to make up for it. If you really want sweets, try to limit yourself to one small sugary/sweet snack per day (or every other day). And, keep the sweet snacks out-of-sight!

Also, limit your intake of foods with added sugars. Examples of things to avoid include:

  • Super-sized soft drinks (sodas)
  • Energy and sports drinks with added sugar
  • Over-sized candy bars
  • Sugar-sweetened fruit drinks
  • Pastries, donuts, cake, cookies
  • Dairy-based desserts

A healthy target, based on World Health Organization guidelines, is to limit your daily added (or "free") sugar intake to 5 to 10% of your total calories (energy) intake. So, for example, if your goal for daily calories intake, is 2,400 calories, your added sugar intake should be not more than 120 to 240 calories (30 to 60 grams).

Each gram of sugar is 4 calories, and a teaspoon of sugar is about 4 grams (16 calories).

Read food labels to help control your sugar intake. The USDA Nutrition Facts label provides the total amount of sugars (natural and added), but does not list added sugars separately (this will change in the future).

Natural sugars are found mainly in fruit and milk products.
