Good Choices:

  • Look for these words to make smart choices: Zheng – steamed. Jum – poached. Kao - roasted. Shao - barbecued (except high-fat, high-sugar spare ribs)

  • Soups such as wonton and egg drop - lower in calories than other appetizers. (However, they are high in sodium.)

  • Steamed spring rolls or vegetable dumplings. Healthier choice than egg rolls, which are fried.

  • Go for steamed rice rather than fried. Brown rice has more fiber than white rice, but they're about the same in calories.

  • Lean beef, chicken, tofu, fish and seafood that is steamed, poached, broiled or grilled

  • Dishes that contain lots of vegetables in light sauces. "Buddha's Delight" is an example: low calories, steamed tofu and vegetables. Other examples: Moo Goo Gai Pan and shrimp with lobster sauce.

Avoid:

  • Fried appetizers like dumplings and egg rolls. An order of dumplings could be 500 calories or more.

  • Lo mein. chow fun and chow mein - fried noodle dishes high in calories (800 calories or more) and sodium

  • General Tso's Chicken, sesame chicken, lemon chicken, and sweet and sour chicken or pork - main dishes that are deep-fried with battered meat and served with high-calorie sauces. Not unusual to get 1,200 calories or more from one of these choices.

  • Crab Rangoon - breaded and deep-fried with a sugary sauce, plus cream cheese.
