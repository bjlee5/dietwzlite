Each serving = NEGATIVE 1 point

Sugary drinks are sweetened with sugar or high-fructose corn syrup, with few other nutrients. Soft drinks, fruit drinks, fruit smoothies and lemonade are examples.

Better alternatives:

  • Water!
  • Low-fat milk
  • Unsweetened coffee or tea

Limit consumption of sugary drinks on a regular basis, since regular consumption can lead to weight gain and Type 2 diabetes. A healthy target, based on World Health Organization guidelines, is to limit your daily added (or "free") sugar intake to 5 to 10% of your total calories (energy) intake. So, for example, if your goal for daily calories intake, is 1,800 calories, your added sugar intake should be not more than 90 to 180 calories (23 to 46 grams).

Each gram of sugar is 4 calories, and a teaspoon of sugar is about 4 grams (16 calories).

Read food labels to help control your sugar intake. The USDA Nutrition Facts label provides the total amount of sugars (natural and added), but does not list added sugars separately (this will change in the future).

Natural sugars are found mainly in fruit and milk products.
