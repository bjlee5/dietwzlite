Each serving = 1 point (whether part of a meal or separate)

A minimum of 2 servings are recommended each day.

Single serving examples:

  • 1 cup raw fruit
  • 1/2 cup dried fruit
  • 1 cup 100% fruit juice

Fruit should always be part of a meal. Eat a variety of fruits – not just one or two types.

Go for fresh, frozen, canned (with no added sugar) or dried fruit.

Fruits such as apples, pears, peaches, plums and grapes have more fiber if you eat the peels. Whole fruits have more fiber than juices.

Fruits and fruit products are good sources of fiber, potassium and magnesium.

Avoid fruits in light or heavy syrups – they add sugar and calories. Go for unsweetened fruits in natural juices. Look for 100% fruit juice.

Eat fruits:

  • As snacks or desserts
  • In salads
  • In place of sugars, syrups or other sweet toppings
