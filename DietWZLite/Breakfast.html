Normal: 1 Point
Light: 2 Points
Excessive: 0 Points
Skip: NEGATIVE 1 Point (Don’t skip meals – it’s not healthy.)

What’s “normal”? A meal with about 1/3 of your daily requirement for calories and daily recommended nutrients.

Light: About 1/4 of your daily calories requirement, but still with some of your daily recommended nutrients. Low-fat and low-calorie choices are usually part of a light meal.

Excessive: More than 1/3 of your daily calories requirement.

Many nutritionists feel breakfast is the most important meal of the day. A healthy breakfast gives you a good start on the day and gives you more flexibility in your meal and beverage choices the rest of the day.

A healthy breakfast should include fiber and protein. (Most people only get about 20% of their daily protein at breakfast - shoot for more than that.) Fiber slows the digestive process so that you feel fuller for a longer period of time (and will be less likely to crave food between meals).

Fruits and veggies should always be part of a meal, but count them separately in DietWZ. Each serving is one point!

Drink a of glass water BEFORE you eat. It makes you feel fuller and aids digestion.

Need help determining your calories requirements? The BEST source for nutrition information is a registered dietitian or your physician. If you can’t do that, check out the DietWZ website.
