Good Choices:

  • Hot and sour shrimp soup which is broth based (but a bit high in sodium)

  • Fresh vegetable spring rolls, which are steamed

  • Use two tablespoons or fewer of sweet and sour sauce - low in calories, but high in sodium

  • Som Tum salad with green papaya

  • Satay skewers, which are grilled.

  • Num Tok marinated grilled beef over greens

  • Cashew chicken for lean protein and healthy fats from the cashews. Eat a reasonable serving and take home leftovers. For a little more fiber, order with brown rice instead of steamed white rice.

  • Pad Thai and other stir-fry dishes - healthy because they use fresh vegetables, noodles, lean proteins and no coconut milk, but easy to over-eat because of portion sizes. Take some home!

  • Pik Pow dishes which have lean protein like shrimp or squid and vegetables.

  • Pra Ram Long Song chicken breast with steamed vegetables. Ask for the peanut sauce on the side.

  • Fruit ice for dessert (mixed fruit in syrup with ice)

Avoid:

  • Coconut milk - which has 400 calories and 50 grams of saturated fat per cup. Curries are often made with coconut milk.

  • Sauces that are high in sodium, and most used in Thai dishes are. Keep this in mind as you look at your sodium intake for the day.

  • Items that are deep fried or covered with greasy or oily sauces

  • Coconut shrimp soup, which has lots of saturated fat. Hot and sour soup is a better choice.

  • Fried spring rolls

  • Coconut ice cream
