Japanese food is generally healthy because few foods are fried and the emphasis is on lean proteins and fresh vegetables.

Good Choices:

  • Seaweed salad (Sunomono) or cucumber salad. Some Japanese restaurants also offer spinach ositashi.

  • Edamame which is baby soybeans full of fiber and protein. Avoid stir-fry servings which are made with oils.

  • Miso soup - for a low-calorie appetizer made with broth, seaweed and tofu, but be aware of its high sodium content (typically 800 mg per cup) as you look at your daily diet.

  • Drink green tea instead of sake, a potent rice wine that adds calories to a meal.

  • Sushi - a Japanese specialty based on boiled rice flavored with sweetened rice vinegar. There are variations including those with raw fish or various chopped vegetables. (Avoid any creamy dipping sauces.)

  • Sashimi - sliced raw fish served with a variety of condiments

  • Yakitori - grilled chicken on small skewers

  • Gyoza - pot stickers (ask for them steamed)

  • Entrees with lots of vegetables and lean proteins, such as fish, tofu and chicken

Avoid:

  • Hibachi dishes which have some basic healthy ingredients but usually have lots of oil or sauce that result in lots of calories.

  • Battered and fried items such as tempura or katsu, or fried rice

  • Spicy rolls which usually have a mayonnaise-based sauce

  • Udon noodles which have high sodium and are made from refined flour. Ask if whole-grain udon noodles are available.
