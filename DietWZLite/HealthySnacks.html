Nearly everyone likes to snack, but snacks can destroy your diet success - particularly if you’re trying to lose weight. However, snacks don’t have to be unhealthy.

A healthy snack is generally one which:

  • Has some nutrition value (and contributes to your overall nutrition objectives)
  • Is low-fat
  • Is low-sugar
  • Is moderate in carbohydrates (carbs)
  • Doesn’t push you over your daily calories intake objective
  • Helps you make it to the next meal without additional snacking

Check out the DietWZ Database of Healthy Food and Beverage Ideas (via “Info”) when you need help with a snack choice.

Tip: Make sure healthy snacks are easy to see in your kitchen and your home. (Hide the unhealthy ones.) When looking for a snack, most people will choose one of the first things they see.

Your body needs carbohydrates as a source of energy, but too many carbs is unhealthy. The DGA recommends you keep your daily carbs intake in the range of 45 to 55% of your daily total calories intake. And, go for healthy carb sources, such as whole grains, vegetables, fruits and beans.

DGA is the USDA/USDHHS 2015 - 2020 Dietary Guidelines for Americans. 8th Edition. December 2015
