Greek and Middle Eastern (Syria, Morocco, Lebanon, Turkey, Israel and Armenia) foods are part of the healthy Mediterranean Diet that includes an emphasis on vegetables and fruit, heart-healthy olive oil, beans and whole grains.

Good Choices:

  • Dolmas (also called dolmades) - grape leaves stuffed with a savory or sweet well-seasoned filling. Choose fillings that contain more vegetables than meat.

  • Hummus - made with chickpeas, tahini, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil and other flavorings, is rich in protein. Try it with fresh vegetables, instead of pita bread, to save a few more calories.

  • Baba ghanoush - a dip similar to hummus, but pureed roasted eggplant is substituted for the chickpeas.

  • Avgolemono - a lemon-egg yolk sauce used with many dishes including soup. It is a good choice in place of rich cheese sauces.

  • Tzatziki - a healthy sauce (or thicker like a dip) made with yogurt, mint or dill, chopped cucumbers, garlic, olive oil and vinegar. Another good option with fresh vegetables for dipping instead of pita bread.

  • Tabbouleh (tabuli) - a dish of cracked bulgur wheat tossed with chopped tomatoes, onions, parsley, mint, olive oil and lemon juice. It is served cold as a salad or appetizer.

  • Souvlaki - kabobs (kebabs) made of chicken, lamb or pork. A good low-fat option when skewered with healthy vegetables.

  • Kakavia soup - a broth-based soup with fish, shellfish, vegetables and herbs

  • Gyro and shawarma - spiced, minced lamb, thin sliced, tucked into a pita and topped with grilled onions and sweet peppers. This is a healthy sandwich.

Limit portions:

  • Pita bread

  • Lavash (lavosh) - a round, thin crisp bread also known as Armenian cracker bread

  • Sutlac - rice pudding topped with cinnamon

Avoid:

  • Falafel - a deep-fried blend of ground fava beans or chick peas, herbs and spices that is formed into croquettes

  • Phyllo (filo) - a tissue-thin pastry dough, often brushed with butter

  • Spanokopita pie or turnovers made with phyllo dough and a filling of spinach, onions, feta cheese, eggs and seasonings

  • Moussaka - a baked, layered casserole of sliced eggplant and ground lamb or beef covered with a rich egg and cheese sauce. Itâ€™s the Greek version of lasagna.

  • Pastitsio - a baked casserole with pasta (usually macaroni), ground beef or lamb, grated cheese and tomatoes seasoned with cinnamon and other spices, covered with rich egg and cheese sauce.

  • Baklava - the most famous Mediterranean dessert, consists of many butter-drenched layers of phyllo pastry, spices and nuts. Spiced honey-lemon syrup is poured over the warm, baked pastry and allowed to soak in before cutting into squares or triangles and serving. (Hard to resist - share it!)
