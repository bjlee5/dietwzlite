Good Choices:

  • French Onion soup (Soupe a l'oignon), but served without the crusty bread and melted cheese topping

  • Coq au Vin - chicken simmered in red or white wine

  • French salads are among the best in any cuisine, and are excellent choices for healthy dining. Ask for the dressing on the side.

  • Salad Nicoise - a combination that includes tomatoes, black olives, garlic, olive oil and anchovies plus additions such as French green beans, onions, tuna, hard-cooked eggs and herbs.

  • Boeuf Bourguignonne or Daube - beef simmered in red wine

  • Veal Marengo - stew with tomatoes and mushrooms

  • Steak au Poivre - a pan-seared steak encrusted with peppercorns and served in a cognac sauce

  • Roasted or pan-seared meats and poultry

  • Fish Provencal- poached or broiled, with olives, garlic, tomatoes and herbs

  • Although Coquilles St. Jacques contains scallops in cream sauce with cheese, the portions served are usually small (appetizer size) so this is a good choice if you want a taste of a French sauce with your dinner.

  • Slice of light, chewy baguette (French bread)

  • Bouillabaisse - a broth-based seafood stew with onion, tomatoes, garlic, olive oil and herbs

  • Ratatouille - a lighter vegetable casserole made with eggplant, tomatoes, onions, peppers and zucchini with herbs

  • Cassoulet - a long-cooking casserole containing a variety of meats including duck confit (preserved duck), lamb, bacon, pork loin, sausage and white beans in herbs. The large portion of meat makes this a high-fat entree - share it with a fellow diner!

  • Haricot verts - lightly buttered young French green beans

  • Healthier dessert options: French chefs do a tremendous job with fruits poached in a variety of syrups and liqueurs. They are sweet and satisfying without the excessive calorie count.

Avoid:

  • Caneton (duck) a l'orange - roasted duck served with a sweetened sauce.

  • Chicken or Veal Cordon Bleu - meat stuffed with ham and cheese that is breaded and fried in butter

  • Veal Prince Orloff - veal roast stuffed with rice and other ingredients such as cream sauce

  • Langoustine en Croute - lobster in buttery puff pastry

  • Escargot (snails) while low in fat, are served with butter, so choose carefully.

  • Limit cheese, butter and cream by staying away from quiches, souffles and Alsatian Tart

  • Croissants - buttery rolls that are high in calories

  • Vol-au-Vent - a buttery pastry shell with a lid, filled with a rich, creamy mixture of chicken, fish or meat and vegetables

  • En croute - any dish wrapped in a rich pastry

  • Vichyssoise - a chilled cream of potato soup.

  • Pate (also called terrine) - a finely ground mixture of meats, fat and seasonings

  • Foie gras - goose liver with very high fat content

  • Pommes Anna - thinly sliced layers of heavily buttered potatoes that are baked

  • Pommes Dauphine - potato puree combined with puff pastry dough then deep fried

  • Pommes Frites (French fries) - just as high, or higher in calories than those served at fast-food chains

French cuisine is known for its very rich desserts that are not only high in calories but contain large amounts of sugar and fat. These desserts are hard to resist. If you can't resist, consider sharing with a friend!
