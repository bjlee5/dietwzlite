Each serving = 1 point (whether part of a meal or separate)

A minimum of 2 servings are recommended each day.

Examples of a single serving of vegetables:

  • 1 cup raw or cooked vegetables
  • 1/2 cup dried vegetables
  • 1 cup vegetable juice
  • 2 cups leafy salad greens

Most people need to increase their vegetables intake. Eat a variety of vegetables, particularly red and orange colored vegetables, beans and peas. For adults, 2-1/2 cups per day is a good target to start with.

Examples of different types of vegetables:

  • Red and orange : peppers, tomatoes, carrots, sweet potatoes, winter squash, pumpkin.
  • Starchy : corn, white potatoes, green peas
  • Other : iceberg lettuce, green beans, onions
  • Beans or peas : kidney beans, lentils, chickpeas, pinto beans (great protein sources!)
  • Dark-green : spinach, broccoli, romaine, collard, turnip and mustard greens

    • Vegetables are a good source of dietary fiber, potassium, folate, magnesium and vitamins A, C and K and protein!

    Raw, cut-up veggies make great snacks!
